            Moonlight Greatsword is a Weapon in Dark Souls and Dark Souls Remastered.

             

            This sword, one of the rare dragon weapons, came from the tail of Seath the Scaleless, the pale white dragon who betrayed his own."
            "Seath is the grandfather of sorcery, and this sword is imbued with his magic, which shall be unleashed as a wave of moonlight."

             

            How to Get / Where to Find the Moonlight Greatsword

            • Acquired by cutting off Seath the Scaleless' tail.
            • Out of the three tails, player must cut the middle one, by hitting the end of the tail until it comes off. Make sure you are lightweight enough to run quickly. Run over and stand by the crystal you need to destroy in order to damage the boss at the start of the fight, and wait for him to shuffle over and do a breath attack. Wait for his attack to end in a few seconds, then shatter the crystal which will stun him giving you plenty of time to chop or Soul Spear his tail off. The tail should fall off after about 500 damage.
            • After patch 1.05, the sword gets directly transfered into your inventory, so it is no longer possibile to lose it for the playthrough, after cutting Seath's tail and leaving it's tip containing the weapon inside the boss arena. You will acquire the weapon, even without finishing off Seath.

             

            Hints and Tips:

            • Can be given to Kingseeker Frampt to receive 1 Soul.
            • Moonlight Greatsword can be upgraded by using Dragon Scales.
            • The Moonlight Great Sword's heavy attacks fling ethereal blades of magic at the enemy, which explode on impact. Each swing of this ability reduces durability of the weapon by 20-40 points, depending on whether you are wielding it one or two handed. These ranged heavy attacks have heavy knock-back, sending foes flying or knocking them down upon impact.
            • Wielding the sword raises Magic defense by 40.
            • The Intelligence damage scaling has greatly reduced effectiveness past 40 Intelligence. Up to and including 40 Intelligence, each point of Intelligence adds about 7 damage with a fully upgraded Moonlight Greatsword. Above 40 Intelligence, each point of Intelligence adds about 2 damage with the fully upgraded sword.
            • Pure magic damage, useful moveset and fairly low stat requirements make Moonlight Greatsword one of the most popular PvP weapons.

             

            Lore

            • This sword is part of a long line of Moonlight Greatswords, dating back to the 1994 King's Field game developed by From Software.

                                                So much of the comments are: how to cut tale??? And me cut tail ez. So heres some helpful info for using this weapon.
                                                First of all the durability cost listed for using the r2 2 handed is actually 80 not 40. Its only 40 1 handed. That being said you should keep repair powder in your hotbar and use it frequently if you want to spam blast attacks. However this isnt a mindless spam weapon, as you will find people rolling through or blocking your projectiles if not timed properly. Advanced users will lock, wind up the r2 and then unlock to free aim at targets. It is far more reliable to wind up your r2 while someone recovers from a backstab/riposte, especially 2 handed. This will often instakill most players, as they must roll/block the projectile, the blast and your sword almost all at once. You will be surprised how the moonlight blast can sneak between iframes.
                                                Another approach is firing down narrow hallways or corridors, or charging the r2 around a corner to hit a target as they enter line of sight. With the proper angle you can still hit the moving host with the blast, forcing them to panic roll into a wall.
                                                Then there is of course cliffs. Because this weapons r2 will often blow people backwards, you can abuse the rafters in anor londo, the bridges and ledges in places like the catacombs etc. Even if they block it they will often still get pushed back far enough to die. Since you are running int for this, i recommend logans catalyst, pursuers and if necessary power within for dealing with 3 enemies at once. When a player triggers your pursuers that is the time to start free aiming the r2 for a rollcatch. If they are stupid enough to stand and try to block the pursuers, then this will usually kill them.
                                                Finally. Dont be afraid to let loose at the back of a fleeing host. Its not easy moving the camera especially on consoles. They will never see it coming and panic roll into the blast. Moonlight greatsword is more fun than meta to be honest. I see mages everywhere wielding it but they always forget the potential of the r2. I hope this reminds you.
