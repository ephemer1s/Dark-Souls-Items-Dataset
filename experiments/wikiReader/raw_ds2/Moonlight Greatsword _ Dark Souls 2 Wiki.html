            Moonlight Greatsword is a weapon in Dark Souls 2.

             

            The blade of this greatsword shines like the brilliant rays of the moon.

            In the oldest legends, rarely spoken of today, it is said that the sword was born of a great white being.

            The strong attack unleashes its strength, launching a wave of moonlight.

            Effect: special attack (strong attack)

            Acquired From

            • Boss Soul trade with Weaponsmith Ornifex, requires Old Paledrake Soul and 10,000 souls.

             

            Hints and Tips:

            • Upgraded with Petrified Dragon Bones.
            • Cannot be buffed anymore as of 1.03 patch.
            • Possesses a unique heavy attack both one handed and two handed: 1h is a horizontal magic beam with a vertical follow-up ground smash. 2h's are a vertical magic beam with a horizontal follow-up. A power-stance heavy attack will not fire this beam unlike some other special attacks.
            • This attack costs 10 durability to use, however the Bracing Knuckle Ring and its variants reduce this cost to 8, 7 and 5 respectively. With the required 18 int to wield this weapon, the Repair spell can be used to increase the number of uses of this attack without needing to buy Repair Powders or sitting at a bonfire.
            • From 18 to 38 int, the Raw infusion wins over both the Regular and Magic infusions. Above 39, the Magic Infusion becomes the strongest. (448 AR Magic vs 488 AR Raw at 18 int, 512 AR Magic vs 511 AR Raw at 39 int)
            • The Enchanted infusion converts the weapon’s magic damage to physical, resulting in the heavy attack dealing no damage, as its damage scales entirely off your magic AR. Strangely enough, this also raises your physical block from 60% to 93,3%. (425 physical AR at 18 int, 445 AR at 39 int)

             

            Trivia:

            • The Moonlight Greatsword is a recurring item in FROM Software's games appearing in Ninja Blade, King's Field, Armored Core, Enchanted Arms, Otogi, Demon's Souls, Dark Souls and Bloodborne.
            • This particular instance physically resembles the Demons Souls version of the sword, although there are references to Seath from the original Dark Souls as the originator of this particular sword.
            • It is called by most players with an acronym: MLGS (MoonLight GreatSword).

                                                                              "Power stance heavy attack does not shoot projectiles. you may wield BlueMoon Greatsword on left hand instead for a variable offhand weapon moveset." This needs a correction. While power stance-ing with the MoonLight GreatSword in the left hand, the weapon will not perform the projectile attack. releasing power stance restores this attack. Holding the MoonLight GreatSword in the right hand while power stance-ing does not overwrite the projectile attack, regardless of which weapon is in the left hand. Power Stance move sets are determined by the right hand weapon, and the weapon wielded in the left hand does not change the move set. So long as that weapon is power stance compatible, regardless of whether the offhand is the BlueMoon GreatSword (the fake obtained from Benhart of Jugo) or any other GreatSword, the move set will not change. The claim that a variable offhand move set when wielding both blue swords is possible is completely false. I've just tested it in my game, and I have enough stats to power stance both weapons - but the move set with the MoonLight GreatSword and the BlueMoon GreatSword is exactly identical to the move set of the MoonLight GreatSword and the GreatSword. Players hoping to combine these weapons should be aware that they will receive no benefits.
