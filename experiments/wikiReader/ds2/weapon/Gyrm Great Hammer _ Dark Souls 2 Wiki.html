            Gyrm Great Hammer is a weapon in Dark Souls 2.





            A great hammer used by the nomadic Gyrm.
             
            A makeshift weapon created from a blacksmith's anvil.
            As heavy as it appears.
             
            Crafted rather shoddily, but each hit landed is certain to inflict serious damage. 

            Acquired From

            • Dropped by Gyrm Soldier (Hammer type)

             

            Hints and Tips:

            • Upgraded with regular Titanite. Cannot be infused with Bleed.
            • The R1 attacks have short reach, but they're quick for the weapons' size. One-handed R1 is slam right in front of you. It can hit multiple enemies if they're grouped tightly enough. Two-handed R1 is a spin followed by a slam.
            • The hammer's strong attacks are essentially all spinning moves that hit around you; the two-handed spin will inflict two hits at least--but it takes a large amount of your own stamina with each hit. The two-handed R2 strong attack will inflict massive damage, making it one of the most hard hitting weapons in the game.

                  So... I was comparing greathammer stats. It turns out that this anvil on a stick is just a straight up better version of both the Demon Greathammer and Drakekeeper Greathammer - on paper, anyhow. All 3 of them have the exact same stats except - same damage, defenses, stability, counter damage, and poise damage - but they differ in weight and requirements. The Demon Greathammer requires 50 strength and weighs 26.0; The Drakekeeper requires 50 Strength and weights 20.0; The Gyrm Hammer requires 40 Strength and weighs 20.0; in conclusion, gyrm hammer is just statistically better, requiring 10 less Strength (5 less if you two-hand), having the lowest Dex requirement, and tying with Drakekeeper for weight. Unless the Demon Hammer and Drakekeeper Hammer have better movesets or stamina consumption, there is really no statistical reason to *not* pick Gyrm Hammer over them. Then again, this is based off of the wiki's recorded information, which could potentially be wrong. Some double-checking is necessary,
