            Illusory Ring of a Conqueror

            A ring of blue stone. The stone is misshaped, however imperceptibly. renders certain equipment invisible. Do not judge your foes by their appearance. He who approaches without a sword carries two behind his back.

            Illusory Ring of a Conqueror is a Ring in Dark Souls 2 (DKS2). Players can equip up to 4 Rings, but equipping two of the same item is not possible. Some rings have upgraded versions, having a maximum of +3. Some can only be found in NG+. Rings can be repaired at a blacksmith, but not upgraded. Upgraded versions must be acquired throughout the game from corpses, chests, bosses, etc.

             

            Illusory Ring of a Conqueror Effects

            Durability: 130

            Weight:

            • 0.2

             

             

            • Left hand weapon becomes invisible

             

             

             

            Illusory Ring of a Conqueror Locations

             

            • Clear game without dying. This includes dying while wearing Rings of Life Protection, or while a phantom in another player's world.
            • After clearing the game without dying, you should obtain the ring after the credits.
            • DO NOT SKIP THE CREDITS. Many players have cleared the game with the requirements but have not obtained the rings. It is not yet confirmed, but it seems likely that skipping the credits may actually stop you from getting the rings. To be safe, watch the whole credits after finishing the game.
            • It is possible to back up your save every once in a while and reload it should you die if you just want the ring and don't care for the challenge.

             

             

             

            Notes

             

            • A good strategy to achieve this is to complete it in a NG+ or higher world where your gear is already endgame, preventing death from early bosses that may prove challenging with early game weapons and providing high amounts of Estus and healing items from the previous play through.

                                      I decided that the best way to prepare for this if you intend to get it by NG+ is to would be through the following. Use an Agape Ring if you are concerned about Soul Memory, and are intent to show off with your invisible rings by the NG+ endgame. Otherwise, enjoy all the extra souls this is sure to generate if you'd rather be simply OP.

                                      Before everything below, gather a bunch of bonfire ascetics via the Pilgrim of the Dark covenant. Enemies there drop Ascetics like candy. You'll want that Dragon's Chime reward as well for what's coming up later, so be sure to complete its journey to Rank 3.

                                      With a mass of bonfire ascetics at hand, proceed....

                                      You'll need restoration consumables. Create a sizable stock of Crimson Waters by hunting out the Shrine of Amana (enter the Champion's Covenant once they're hunted to extinction to continue). Not only will you pick up plenty of twinkling titanite, you'll amass what your primary replacement for both bonfires and Estus. Simple enough. Just do as your playstyle dictates. How you do it is up to you and your normal playstyle.

                                      Optionally, you'll also want to consider Divine Blessings. This can be acquired infinitely by using a bonfire ascetic at the Giant Lord. All you need to do at minimum is grab the replacement ascetic and the Divine Blessing at his location. If you are interested in farming his souls while you're at it, I recommend using a Soul Vessel to temporarily turn yourself into a STR based monster with some really heavy power stanced hammers.

                                      With the Bonfire Ascetics, Crimson Waters, (and optionally) Divine Blessings amassed....
                                      ~FINALLY, THE FUN PART OF PREPARATION~&lt;3

                                      --It is time to farm the Old Iron King (again, use an Agape Ring if your concerned about soul memory)--

                                      ++For best results, I recommend using a Soul Vessel to temporarily turn yourself into a 99 FTH super cleric armed with a +5 lightning infused Dragon Chime and Heide Lance++

                                      Buff yourself with Denial (incase his wonky hitboxes gives him lucky blow that'll otherwise one-shot you) and Sunlight Blade. Spam the Heavenly Thunder spell (picked up from Cromwell and Blue Sentinel Tar***) while poking him with your pointy stick. The push over will start to go down within 4 minutes a fight.

                                      [Pro-Tip: If interested in the souls, then I recommend dumping all your helmets save for your primary helmet and the Symbol of Avarice into your item box. Quick swap to the Symbol every time you defeat him, but DO NOT WEAR IT DURING THE FIGHT as it will kill you wherever your Denial spell would have saved you (and you'll NEED Denial given the occasions Old Iron King's hitboxes might screw you over). This hot-swap, combined with the best soul gear (Silver Covenant Ring +2, Jester's Gloves, Tseladora Trossers, and the Nahr Alma Chestpiece), you'll net ~325k per run]

                                      You are now prepared for the run. You have what it takes to avoid using bonfires, and minimized the chance of death by skipping half the game using Old Iron's Boss Souls to bypass the Shrine of Winter. Perhaps you're just plain OP as well, had you chosen to prepare by absorbing your kills.

                                      Now make a beeline for Nashandra. Good luck!
