            Farron Greatsword is a Weapon in Dark Souls 3.



            Greatsword of the Abyss watchers which is atypically paired with a dagger.

            The dagger is utilized as a wedge in the left hand while the greatsword is held in the right, a unique technique that was synonymous with the Undead Legion. Confounds foes the manner of wolves hunting prey. 

            Skill: Parry
            Deflect an attack when timed properly and follow up with a critical hit, executed with the dagger.



            Notes and Tips: 

            • Can't be Buffed or Infused.
            • Does 20% bonus damage against Abyssal foes.
              • Darkwraith
              • Deep Accursed
              • Harald Legion Knight
              • Pus of Man
              • Rotten Flesh
              • Iron Dragonslayer (Needs confirmation)
              • High Lord Wolnir
              • Aldrich, Devourer of Gods
              • Dragonslayer Armour
              • Darkeater Midir
            • Upgraded with Titanite Scales.
            • The moveset when dual wielding has attacks similar to the Abyss Watchers, and has a 3-attack cycle like all other dual weapons.
            • You can chain R1 and L1 attacks. For example: If you do two R1 attacks and then an L1 attack, you'll do the third L1 attack.
            • The Parry has identical parry frames to a medium shield. Same Startup, 2 more active, and 2 less recovery than medium shield (30fps).
            • The L1/LB attack is not affected by the weight value.
            • Since it doesn't require FP to be used, it can be handy for low level builds without any investment on Attunement.
            • Has an A scaling in DEX when fully upgraded which is unusual for an Ultra Greatsword.
            • Regardless of being wielded by either "one-hand" or "two-hands (with off-hand dagger)," weapon attacks will be treated as being "one-handed" along with any of the associated properties of "one-handedness" (i.e.: attacks can still be parried, attacks will still be deflected by shields, no bonus AR from STR, etc.).
            • Has an unused Weapon Skill in which the wielder poses a stance and thrust similar to the Stomp skill, this is replaced by the Parry.
            • Both the dagger and the greatsword can be parried.
            • The farron greatsword is ironically not compatible with the legion etiquette emote, meaning the dagger will disappear when it is performed 

             

            Softcaps & Scaling:

            • 18/20 = 375 AR = Base Stats
            • 40/20 = 449 AR = Str Softcap
            • 18/40 = 492 AR = Dex Softcap
            • 40/40 = 566 AR = Quality Build 

             

            Location/Where to Find:

            • Created with Soul of the Blood of the Wolf

             

            Lore

            • The Abyss Watchers stem from Artorias The Abysswalker.

                                                                                                                    Actually a solid weapon. Once you get a feel for the L1s, you can mix them up with R1s as they combo into each other; this makes you harder to parry due to the fact the L1 is far slower to connect fully than the R1, but covers more distance, has a melee dagger slash that catches parries too close, and better roll-catching ability.

                                                                                                                    Some gankers/noobs also simply don't know how to counter it and stand dumbly while they get hit by it. Just DON'T do the 3rd L1, it's easy to parry with no skill required and anything from a Caestus to a medium shield. If your opponent doesn't have a parry tool ready, it is still easy to roll away from or backstab punish. If you want to put the pressure on and have plenty of stamina left, follow up L1 -&gt; L1 -&gt; R1. It'll be too fast to parry if they were stunlocked by the first 2 L1s, so they'll either get hit or roll away.

                                                                                                                    While this weapon can't really trade with other ultras, not least because dex leaves fewer stat points to invest into vitality due to the higher softcap, it's not really meant to. With good timing, you can actually use an L1 to hit an overhead attacking ultra while also dodging their R1 from the spin. Play it more like a regular greatsword, going for trades against smaller weapons, and whiff punishes against larger ones. Yeah, it's no meta weapon, but this thing is reasonably powerful as long as you don't just mindlessly spam L1.
