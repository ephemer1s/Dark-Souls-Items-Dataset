            Greatsword

            		 Strike/Thrust

            Blade of Peril

            		 8(17/17)

            Gael's Greatsword is a Weapon in Dark Souls 3 The Ringed City DLC.



            Greatsword of Slave Knight Gael, the only weapon that he kept with him from beginning to end.

            Originally an executioner's sword made for decapitation, this blade is heavily chipped and stained with the blood of countless battles.

            Skill: Blade of Peril
            A precarious technique unique to undead Gael. Leap in any direction, slamming the greatsword to the ground, then follow with normal attack for a large spinning slash, or strong attack to back-step and jump foward in an overhead slam.



            Notes and Tips:

            • Not buffable or infusable. 

            • A true combo for this weapon (while two-handing) is R1+L2+R1.
            • Upgrades with Titanite Scales.
            • Gael's Greatsword +5 has 504 AR at 40 strength and 40 dexterity.
            • The sword resembles a broken version of the Executioner's Greatsword. Before the boss fight with Slave Knight Gael, while he is still an NPC, he uses an Executioner's Greatsword.
            • The spin part of Blade of Peril (L2+R1) can be parried.
            • If you have a shield that allows for the use of weapon skills, the shield will still be held as you perform the weapon art.

             

             

            Softcaps & Scaling:

            • 2 Handed Stats: 
              • 19/13 = 392 AR = Base Stats
              • 30/13 = 448 AR = Str Softcap
              • 19/39 = 452 AR = Dex Softcap
              • 40/13 = 463 AR
              • 19/60 = 464 AR
              • 19/99 = 479 AR = Dex Hardcap
              • 66/13 = 495 AR = Str Hardcap
              • 28/39 = 505 AR = Combined Softcap
              • 40/40 = 525 AR
              • 66/40 = 556 AR
              • 66/99 = 583 AR = Combined Hardcap
            • After softcaps it gets less than 2 AR per level
            • 2 handed Quality weapon
              • Scales well with both stats
              • Gains more from Strength than Dexterity

             

             

            Location/Where to Find

            • Obtained via Soul Transposition from the Soul of Slave Knight Gael for 20,000 souls.

                                                                                                                                                          Gael's Greatsword 101 - This weapon fits well on a variety of builds. Generally though, your STR should be greater than or equal to your DEX with this weapon. You should be two handing this weapon most of the time. Although, the one handed R1s can be useful as they swing horizontally. This weapon has arguably the best true combo in the game (a series of hits that are inescapable in PVP every time if done correctly... and without lag): R1, weapon art, R1. While the weapon art does consume FP, it, and therefore the true combo, can be executed without FP. There is no reduction in damage output, however, the weapon art will consume more stamina. The true combo should be used after every successfully landed two handed R1 with few exceptions. The weapon art also has an R2 follow-up, but it's very bad and you should never use it. Even if it is super cool looking. The two handed R2 is also extremely good and should not be over looked. Since it can be charged by holding down R2, the opponent won't be quite sure when the attack will initiate. Fully charged R2s hit opponents surprisingly often, which deals devastating damage. Because the two handed R2s are so hard hitting and their timing is unpredictable, people are usually wary while you're charging it, so it's fairly low risk, too. This weapon has decent absorption and stability, so don't be too worried about needing to block with it from time to time. While this weapon is quite well rounded, it's somewhat slow speed can make it a bit sluggish when trying to hit fleeing opponents, so a faster, lighter weapon in your off-hand is a nice tool for those situations.
