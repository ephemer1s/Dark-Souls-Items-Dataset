            Pyromancer's Parting Flame is a Weapon in Dark Souls 3.



            The pyromancy flame of Livid Pyromancer Dunnel that attracts the echoes of  death.

            When Dunnel lost his hideous spouse, he gave his own pyromancy flame as an offering, which transformed into a parting flame. Not long after, Dunnel became a mad spirit, cursed to wander the lands.

            Skill: Parting Flame

            Release store of death echoes to gain a modest dose of Estus.

             

            Notes and Tips:

            • Upgraded with normal Titanite, and can only be upgraded by Cornyx of the Great Swamp.
            • The Pyromancer's Parting Flame leaves a trail of fire during its casting animations, which will damage nearby opponents. This additional damage compensates for its lower spell buff when compared to the Pyromancy Flame, and will out damage the Pyromancy Flame on any pyromancy where the fire trail hits as well as the pyromancy. The flame trail can trigger before the pyromancy itself, this is notable on pyromancies like Black Flame as it makes it difficult to dodge at close range. The following is a list of pyromancies that produce a flame trail that can deal additional damage: Fireball, Fire Orb, Bursting Fireball, Great Chaos Fire Orb, Chaos Bed Vestiges, Great Combustion, Sacred Flame, Profaned Flame, Black Flame, Black Fire Orb, Seething Chaos. It should be noted that Sacred Flame does not produce a flame trail, but does deal additional damage as though it did.
            • Has a higher stamina consumption than the regular Pyromancer's Flame.
            • The weapon art's ability is triggered by killing enemies while the flame is active.  After killing 12 enemies, the Parting Flame will begin to glow brightly.  Activating the WA at this point will cause the player to regain 1 normal Estus flask.  The WA must be used before the Flame can accumulate another charge from more deaths, meaning the stored "charge" cannot stack.
            • The Strength requirement not being met has a different effect than other catalysts. Spell buff, Spell damage, Focus cost, Stamina consumption, and the damage from the fire trail all see no penalties for players with less than 15 Str. Lacking the strength requirement, like many weapons, renders the Weapon art unusable.

             

             

            Location/Where to Find

            • Painted World of Ariandel: Past the Snowy Mountain Pass bonfire, on the bottom floor of the crypt with the giant flies (can also be accessed from the Ariandel Chapel bonfire if the shortcut to here has already been opened).  There is a fly facing an illusory wall on the top floor which will lead to the bottom floor.  From here, proceed to the right side of the room to a walled-up doorway.  This is another illusory wall.  Proceed through here until you reach the outside area with giant roots.  Carefully descend down the roots until you reach the tower below.  The corpse here will contain the Parting Flame.

                                                                                                                                                          Actually Parting Flame without meeting the 15 strength requirement only "bounces off" when hitting certain sturdy/tough mobs, usually the ones with obese looking like Winged Knights, Cathedral Knights and Old Demon Knights. Against mobs with standard physique such as Lothric Knights, Silver Knights and Oceiros, Parting Flame works just as good even if the caster does not meet the strength requirement --- --- dealing regular damage and using normal stamina.

                                                                                                                                                          When against a raised shield without meeting the 15 strength requirement, Parting Flame only "bounces off" when the "Flaming hand" directly contacts the raised shield during casting. In other words, even if the enemy raised its shield and the caster does not have 15 strength, as long as the caster avoids to have his/her Parting Flame get in touch with enemy's shield during casting, Parting Flame would work just fine as the enemy does not have its shield raised.

                                                                                                                                                          In PvP, under most cases, Parting Flame would work normally even if the caster does not reach the 15 strength requirement. Parting Flame would only bounce off when directly hits an enemy players's raised shield during casting, or hits an enemy player under the effects of certain weapon arts such as Perseverance or Stone Flesh.

                                                                                                                                                          Hence, if you simply want to use Parting Flame in PvP, it is not mandatory to raise your strength to 15 in order to use Parting Flame effectively in most cases; you just need to be familiar with circumstances that Parting Flame would "bounce off" and avoid to use Parting Flame before equipping Knight's Ring in those conditions.
