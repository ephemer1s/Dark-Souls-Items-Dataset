            Dark Hand is a Weapon in Dark Souls 3.



            Weapon that allows its wielder to evoke an art unique to Londor, the land of the Hollow. It is also said to be an ancient relic of a Primordial Serpent.

            The Dark Hand mercilessly saps the essence of its victims, and can also double as a special shield. Cannot be used two-handed.

            Skill: Lifedrain
            Embrace the victim and steal their HP. Can only be used against humans. 



            Notes and Tips:

            • Cannot be Infused, Buffed or Reinforced.
              • Despite this, it can still be useful in PvE up to mid-game.  It can be obtained as early as after defeating Vordt, and its base damage is very high and is split heavily towards one damage type (Dark).  It also scales with the 4 main damage stats and has no requirements, allowing pretty much any build to use it as a substitute for a Deep- or Dark-infused weapon.
            • Adding to this weapon's uniqueness, it is technically classified as a spell catalyst, despite not functioning as one. 
            • If equipped in right hand, it acts as a striking weapon with a fist-weapon moveset.
              • The right handed light attack seems able to stun-lock PvP players in early-mid game indefinitely. Whether this works at higher levels would need further testing.
              • Its strike property can also be useful for stun-locking the skeletons in the Catacombs of Carthus.  This is especially useful for dexterity builds, which usually have a hard time finding decent weapons with strike damage properties early on in the game.
              • While the weapon cannot be two-handed, it's weapon art can still be used when right-handed by using a weapon art shield in the left hand, or by having your left hand empty.
              • Unlike the DS1 version, its weapon art cannot be parried what so ever.
            • Can be used as a shield when equipped in left hand.
              • Because it doesn't count as a shield, this weapon cannot be buffed with Magic Shield, making it a very bad shield due to the low stability. With 30 Endurance almost any weapon can get through your stamina bar with 2-3 hits.
              • When using a weapon that can attack with the shield up, like a spear or rapier, the shield animation dissapears despite it still blocking damage.
            • This item is missable in a playthrough if not purchased from Yuria of Londor. 
            • This weapon can do more damage with its skill if used behind someone, with the Dark Clutch Ring, was able to do 1267 damage, plus with the Blindfold Mask, Flynn's Ring and Lloyd's Sword Ring and it was able to do even more, with a high enough dark defence the damage will not show.

             

             

            Location/Where to Find

            • Sold by Yuria of Londor for 12,000 souls

                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Putting aside the much lamented twinking pontential, this thing is one of the best weapons in the game IMO. Okay, fine, it has no upgrades, but if you're playing a INT/FTH build the scaling makes up for it pretty nicely. At around 30/30 you're already doing about 100 additional dark damage although after that the scaling drops off significantly but at least for NG, you're a goddamn monster for PVE when you pair this thing with spellcasting.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                    It also has an entertaining tactical advantage for sorcerers in PVP: while two handing a staff the Hand becomes invisible. This can lead to interesting situations where a melee fighter will boldly charge in thinking you have little to no defense at short range. First you pelt him with magic (might hit a few times even depending on the spell) and then at close range you hit him in the face with your stick (Izalith Staff is ideal for this due to the damage and range). He takes a hundred or so points of damage and you roll away. He gets angry and chases, thinking your defense is that crappy rod. But suddenly you move to one handing the staff and POW-POW, a hit or two in the kisser with the Hand. Suddenly he took hundreds of points of dank damage or if he has no parrying implement, you might even be able to spam him to death with punches. Alternatively, you can roll away yourself when he's reeling because hitting with fist weapons costs so little stamina even casters can throw it around effortlessly. And then there's the fun time when you get to put that dark damage in their place where the sun don't shine...
