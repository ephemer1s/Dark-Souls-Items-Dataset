            Estoc is a Weapon in Dark Souls 3.



            Large thrusting sword used for piercing through the armor of enemies.

            The sharp edge of the sword's rock-solid blade can also be used in slashing attacks.

            Skill: Lock onto your target and hit them in a large forward lunge to pierce through enemy shields and inflict staggering damage.

             

            Notes and Tips:

            • Reinforced with Titanite
            • Can be Infused and Buffed



            Location/Where to Find

            • Starting equipment for the Assassin.
            • Sold by Greirat after sending him to pillage the Undead Settlement or the Shrine Handmaid after giving her Greirat's Ashes for 3000 souls.



             

            Moveset and Videos:

            (If your name is not on THIS list, please do not post videos here. If you wish to post videos, please click the link and apply in the thread)

            • Estoc moveset
            • The light attack is a rapid thrust. 
            • The heavy attack is a horizontal slash while advancing. A second press slashes back across the body. 
            • The weapon art is a powerful lunge.
            • The forward light attack is a leap backward while slashing across.

                                                                        I'm writing this post a slight guide for new players, and I'll touch a bit on this weapons good aspects and some of the not so good. So I'm first say that after years of playing with every weapon in the game, this weapon is pretty good. However, it isn't as easy use as a straight sword or a curved sword, and anything that is easy to use lends itself to become even better as your skill and knowledge with the game mechanics increases. Following this logic, this weapon isn't really top tier. It's attack speed tends to be only a frame or two faster than straight swords, but it loses out on range and damage, and has a rather small and finicky hit-box. However it does have a slightly faster recovery, and this leads to one of the first unique aspects of this weapon. Off-hand, like all weapons, it gets a small boost to range. But the quick recovery frames after an attack allows you to combo into ANY main hand weapon attack. This makes the weapon extremely useful for dual wielding slower weapons, as catching an opponent with a hit from this can combo into a hit from a more devastating weapon like a great hammer/axe or ultra greatsword. The slightly faster attack speed allows it to combo AFTER most spells, if the enemy is in range. Some spells with extremely quick recovery (like lightning arrow) actually combo into two R1's. This is due to the fact that there is a kinda of build up mechanic for stunning players, with diminishing periods of being "stunned" as it builds up. Bigger weapons deal more stun "damage" so to speak, and as a result, the stun window after a spell and the first R1 hit is too small to land a second R1 with most weapons. Certain spells and this weapon deal enough stun damage to stun a player, but so little that the stun window is still long enough to land a second R1 even after a spell has landed. However, this weapon cannot land 3 R1's on it's own. Another unique aspect is that the slightly faster attack speed makes registering back stabs more consistent than a straight sword, and the range the weapon posses makes it better than daggers at registering backstabs. Seems to be that registering requires some kind of "check" that is influenced by the weapons attack speed, range, and the opponents position. In short, the range and speed of this weapon just registers backstabs more consistently than the slightly slower straight sword and the much shorter dagger. Personally, I can't give a number to quantify this, but it's definitely noticeable. Even through latency, opponents seem to get "sucked" into a backstab animations quite a bit more often than any weapon I've played with. Lastly I'd like to shed light on two of this weapons attacks in particular as they exceed in some common situations. One, is that while blocking with a shield, you can perform an R1 with reduced range. This is useful for closing with and trading against weapons that might have a slight range or speed advantage (don't try trading with ultra weapons like this, as it's not necessary to block slower weapons you can easily roll). Also, the running attack adds extra running steps when initiated, giving better range and several frames of damage registration in a linear pattern. This is useful for chasing opponents rolling directly away from you. My final opinion is that this weapon works best with with a shield backstab fishing, or off-hand for the increased range and capabilities to combo. On it's own the moveset isn't particularly strong, and as a back up weapon for a caster it's moveset (being mostly linear) just does what casters already do well. I suggest just using a straight sword for the better range, damage, hit registration, and horizontal moveset that seems (IMO) to better compliment your caster's spells. This weapon finds itself on some of my top 10 favorite builds for it's utility, but on it's own I wouldn't place this in my pick for top 10 best weapons.
