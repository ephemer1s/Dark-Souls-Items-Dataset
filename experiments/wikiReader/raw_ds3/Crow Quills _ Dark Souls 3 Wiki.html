            Crow Quills is a Weapon in Dark Souls 3. It is part of the Ashes of Ariandel DLC.



            Thrusting sword wielded by Corvian Knights, and a special paired weapon. When twin-handed, brandish four thin-edge blades in the left hand.

            In their infatuation with Sister Friede, the Corvian Knights swore to protect the painting from fire, and to this end, took to the execution of their own brethren.

            Skill: Quill Dart

            Simultaneously throw temporarily substantial illusions of the four thin-edged blades wielded in the left hand.



            Notes and Tips:

            • Can be Infused and Buffed.
            • Reinforce with Titanite.
            • This weapon is best used on a Dex build.
            • Sharp infusion gets S Dex scaling with 373 AR at 9/40 STR/DEX.
            • This weapon has high critical rating, it is optimal for ripostes/backstabs.
            • When Two-Handing, the left hand backstep and run attacks have a leaping animation.
            • During the Weapon Art, after the quills have been thrown, if R2 is pressed, it triggers a followup thrust, similar to the charge Weapon Art
            • LB/L1 second attack - the triple thrust can also be chained from a single RB/R1. The LB/L1 finisher can be chained from a double RB/R1. All in all the LB/L1 combo is 7 attacks (2 horizontal swipes, 3 thrusts, into a backhand swipe then thrust) 
            • Weapon Art Quills throw in a wide arc, best if used up close to hit all quills



            Location/Where to Find

            • Painted World of Ariandel: From the Corvian Settlement Bonfire head out and across the bridge. Follow the wall of the house on your left until you see a smaller bridge. Go across and follow the path to the left. On your left you will see two entrances across a small gap. jump it and go into the house on your right. Barrels will be blocking a platform that you need to run and leap off of and land on the elevated platform with the Crow Quills.

                                                                                                                                                      Stay away from initiating combos with L1, since the quills themselves have piss-poor range and little stagger. You have to be right up in the opponent's face for them to hit, and even then they do pretty minimal damage at max upgrade and infusion. In fact, be extremely cautious using L1 chains in general, since they leave you wide open to be parried.

                                                                                                                                                      The WA's R2 follow-up is also easy for opponents to parry, since the quills themselves are a dead giveaway as to your intentions. Try throwing multiple volleys in succession before following through (like a bona fide Corvian Knight), but even then you have to be careful the enemy doesn't come bounding after you before you land again, or worse, that they don't spam their Chaos Bed Vestiges.

                                                                                                                                                      It's definitely a tough weapon to master, but I'd say it's fairly rewarding. If you don't like dawdling in drawn-out fights and would rather combo someone under controlled conditions, you'll love the Crow Quills. But, if you're a little overzealous and often forget parrying is a thing like I do when wielding this weapon... Well, hang on to your estoc...
