            Handmaid's Dagger is a Weapon in Dark Souls 3.



            Dagger used by handmaids of the Profaned Capital. Each successful attack replenishes a slight amount of FP.

            It is said that these women took pleasure in wounding others.

            Skill: Blind Spot
            Use against shielded foes to break through their guard by attacking from the side. 




            Notes and Tips:

            • Cannot be Infused or Buffed.
            • Reinforced with Titanite.
            • All weapons have motion values assigned to each attack, which are modifiers to AR that are associated with the attack being used.
              • The Handmaid's Dagger has a motion value of 330 on backstab, 396 on riposte, and 66+385 on Hornet Ring riposte. The motion value of a backstab with a dagger does not change with Hornet Ring.
              • This means that aside from a difference in AR, no infusion is strictly better for ripostes, as all infusions will get the same motion value, with the only difference being AR. Use whatever infusion will net the most damage for the best results.
            • The Handmaid's Dagger has a range of 22.3366xl on the 1h R1 and 25.6165xl on the 2h R1, thus making it the shortest dagger.
            • This dagger restores 1 FP for each succesful hit. This works even if the enemy is blocking.
            • When in the offhand, the moveset of the basic attack changes to a stab, which has a guaranteed 2 hit combo. This also restores 2 FP per hit
            • The pathetic damage of this short weapon makes it rarely used, and the low FP restore on hit makes simple weapons far more valuable.



            Location/Where to Find

            • Dropped by Jailer Handmaid [PC] 4.00% (Can be easy farmed at the "Yhorm the Giant" bonfire)

                                                                                                Because This is a weapon meant for PVE, not PVP. Aside from the Ashen Estus, there are 4 other ways to get back FP. 1st is from consummables that replenish FP, 2nd from simple infusion, third is from Aldrich's Sapphire, and last is from weapons, which exclusively count the Handmaiden's Dagger, and Executioner's Greatsword. You could used consummables, but who really uses them when you have estus? Let's say, you exclusively use a simple caestus/small parry shield alone. That gives you 3.5 FP in 10s. Which means if you're empty on FP, you have to kite an enemy for 100s to get back enough FP for 1 Soulspear. Given that you could potentially die in clutch which usually amounts to less than half the time it takes to get 3.5 FP, this is goddamn pathetic and inefficient. The icing on this turd cake is that if an enemy/boss attacks with an unparryable move, you're basically equipping dead weight. And really, no one uses a ceastus to fight unless you're desperate in PVE or for builds in PVP. It's the same as using short fingernails to separate 2 lego pieces stuck together. On the other hand, the Handmaid's Dagger is a lightweight weapon that gives 1 FP on every single hit. It deals so little damage, because From wants you to get as much FP as you can per enemy. This is basically the FP version of the Sun Princess Ring. It's trying to encourage a semi-agressive playstyle similar to the rally mechanic in Bloodborne. Because it has low weight, you can go for a light build with fast rolls, get 2-3 whiffs in, and dodge away. Now imagine if you combine the Handmaid's Dagger, a simple infused caestus/parry shield, executioner's greatsword, and Aldrich's Ruby. You can basically farm FP back from killing mobs in PVE. And if you add this lot further; Sun Princess Ring, Blessed Weapon, Prayer for Favor from Filianore's Chime, and Replenishment, that would make for one-hell of an interesting no-estus run. Viable challenge for anyone who isn't a hardcore speedrunner or LobosJr.

                                                                                                                  Severely underrated weapon. No, it doesn't do a lot of damage, but that's the point. Its an FP recovery tool, not a killing tool.

                                                                                                                  There are a lot of mobs in the game that are relatively easy to staggerlock and/or are easily dodgable. Smack those enemies around a bit with this thing to restore FP and extend the amount of time you have before needing to use an ashen estus. This is especially handy on casters, who can really appreciate the ability to effectively extend their FP bar before having to use an ashen estus. The rate of recovery is also significantly faster than a maxed-out Simple infusion when you can attack with it, and due to its light weight you can pair it with a Simple infused caestus for a total of only 1 weight, allowing you to increase FP gains by either equipping both at the same time, or swapping to the caestus when not using the dagger. Go even further by holding an executioner's greatsword in your other hand while you murder things with this dagger.

                                                                                                                  An example: it takes a couple seconds for my 12 str/20dex caster to kill a basic, non-threatening corvian in the corvian village on NG, with the dagger using the offhand moveset (recovering 2 fp a hit). By holding an executioner's greatsword in my right hand while doing this, I recover 16 FP. Even without the Dusk Crown Ring on, that's still a charge of, say, Great Chaos Fire Orb every 2 kills. Pretty nice if you ask me!
