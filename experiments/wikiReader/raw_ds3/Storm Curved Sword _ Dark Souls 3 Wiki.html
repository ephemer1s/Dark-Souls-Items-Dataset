            Storm Curved Sword is a Weapon in Dark Souls 3.



            Curved sword imbued with the strength of the Stormdrake.

            The Nameless King, ally of the ancient dragons, fought beside the Stormdrake in countless battles.  When the great beast fell, the king claimed his soul, as was the custom in the age of gods

            Skill: Tornado
            Imbue blade with the wrath of storm in a spinning motion, and follow with a strong attack to bear that wrath upon foes.



            Notes and Tips:

            • Cannot be Infused or Buffed.
            • Upgraded with Titanite Scales.
            • Out ranges every other Curved Sword except the Pontiff Knight Curved Sword when both are used 2h.

             

            Softcaps & Scaling:

            • 14/20 = 270 AR = Base Stats
            • 27/20 = 295 AR = Str Softcap
            • 14/40 = 345 AR = Dex Softcap
            • 27/40 = 370 AR = Combined Softcaps

             

            Location/Where to Find:

            • Craft from the Soul of the Nameless King via Soul Transposition with Ludleth of Courland.

                    Seems like nobody is aware of how it's WA is able to melt a blocking player's stamina with the Knight Slayer ring equipped.

                    Here's some maths for you silly friends. It's all rounded up.

                    Let's use the softcap for endurance for reference. 40 end leads us with 160 points for stamina.
                    We'll try it out with the best medium shield of the game: Black Knight Shield with 69 in stability; that means that for each hit that you connect on the shield, it shaves off roughly 31% of stamina that you'd lose if you had 0 stability.
                    Example:
                    One-handed R1 on SCS at both 30/30 str/dex = 326
                    The shield will recieve 326 stamina point penalty with 0 stability

                    Now that was if you didn't have any armor on you too. Because what you wear also affects the stamina on block. For that we'll use full havel.

                    Now that we have taken that into account, let's do it with the shield stability now.
                    If a one-handed swing from the sword does 326, we take the 326 and divide the remaining % that the shield leaves off along with the damage your armor also absorbs.
                    326 x 0.35 (havel set slash defense) = 114.1 damage
                    114.1 x 0.31 (black knight shield with 69 stability) = 35.4 stamina damage on block

                    NOW FOR THE WEAPON ART

                    Now lost knowledge from everyone, no one quite knows the properties of the Storm Curved Sword weapon art. However it is safe to say that it is quite similar to an R1 followed up with an R2. So vanilla WA does:
                    114.1 x 0.31 = 35.4 (last R1 calc)
                    +
                    341 (R2 uncharged damage) x 0.35 (havel) = 119.4
                    119.4 x 0.31 = 37

                    Here's a good part. There is a hitbox of wind for each swings in the WA. Both does the same damage categorized as standard damage.
                    50 (wind) x 0.353 (standard absorption) = 17.7 x 0.31 = 5.5 x 2 = 11

                    So you get 83 stamina points depleted with the WA.

                    Now for the other good part. When you slap in the Knight Slayer Ring, all blocked hits receive a bonus 20% penalty on every hit against a shielded opponent.

                    So the new calc is:

                    35.4 + 20% = 43
                    +
                    37 + 20% = 44
                    +
                    (5.5 + 20%) x 2 = 15

                    You've just done 102 stamina damage on a player with 160 stamina points with the Black Knight Shield +5.
                    Since 1h/2h R1 true comboes into the WA, you might add that extra other 43 stamina damage (1h) and deal a whopping 145 stamina damage.

                    First swing of the WA is hard to parry and with the wind hitbox at play the stamina depletion penalty will kick in hard, leaving them at a surprising lose-lose situation.

                    Start using this with the Knight Slayer Ring for the true Storm Curved Sword tech.

                                                                This weapon is great for pvp. the AR is not so good as alot of people know but its a good sacrifice for the speed, pretty good roll-catching and crazy awesome weapon art. The WA and r2 are hard to predict parry especially if you've been mostly r1 spamming in a duel so it allows for good attack mix-ups. Also, if someone is really getting on your nerves you can just stand over their body after using the weapon art and use it again when they get up, it catches them almost 90% of the time and its a really cheesy tactic I reserve for cheap parry gods and lightning arrow spammers. Also, twohanded mode on this thing is not that great since I find that the onehanded actually has better range and speed. What I like to do is pair it with a Weapon Skill shield like the Black Knight shield, which helps with blocking ranged attacks and also allowing me to do the weapon art when they don't expect it. Just keep a caestus or another parrying tool in your second offhand slot if you want to parry.
