            Greatlance is a Weapon in Dark Souls 3.



            This lance features a long pole, and was originally intended to be used by knights for jousting.

            This weapon is primarily used in charging attacks, and would require tremendous strength and dexterity to be handled by a mere foot soldier.

            Skill: Charge
            Hold lance at waist and charge at foe. Use strong attack while charging to extend the length of the charge. 

            Notes and Tips:

            • Can be Infused and Buffed.
            • Reinforced with Titanite.
            • It and all other pike-class weapons have a slow attack speed when used one handed that becomes much faster when wielded with two hands.
            • The Greatlance was considered a very poor weapon upon launch, for it had a very slow moveset (this was worsened in one of the game's patches) and lacked poise on its attacks. However, multiple game patches have improved the slow moveset, the lack of poise and the Pike class overall. This makes it a considerably better weapon than it was before.



            Location/Where to Find

            • Found in Lothric Castle near the Dragon Barracks bonfire. From the bonfire, head up the stairs and to the left to find the Greatlance next to a red-eyed Lothric Knight.

                                                                                                                                                                                                        As the same person who commented "Needs Serious Fixing," I feel obligated to report my discoveries with this weapon after such a long time. After cosplaying as Mauldron the Assassin for two cycles of the game I have found two things that are important:1. This weapon MUST be paired with the Leo Ring at all times for it to really shine (going to explain why in a bit), and2. The weapon is extremely defensive and thus should rotate around a defensive buildUsing a greatshield is an excellent idea because the shield poke will be your fastest attack and is not too shabby with the damage which also gets boosted by thrust counter damage. Complimenting the defensive style, you also want to wear heavier armor and bump up vigor and vitality as you can trade hits more often with higher health and more defense. Rings that also boost defense and give more health are recommended and Havel's Ring +1 and above is required for the armor. Finally the Leo Ring boosts the counter thrust damage of the weapon impressively, BUT it seems to activate whenever it wants to. I don't exactly know why but there seems to be two thresholds for counter damage: the first is when an opponent is in an animation before hitting you, and the second is a successful, frame perfect trade. The second scenario is when the Greatlance beats every other weapon in the game by enabling you to rack up damage of numbers near 800-900 on one R1 alone. Combined with a possibility that this weapon might be able to follow up its R1 with a weapon art and you have a weapon that can be really dangerous... on trades. In summary, very good while trading with people and npcs, bad in its slow speed and extremely defensive, "play perfect" style.
