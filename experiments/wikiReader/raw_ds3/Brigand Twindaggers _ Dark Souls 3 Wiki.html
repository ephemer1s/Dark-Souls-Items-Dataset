            Brigand Twindaggers are a Weapon in Dark Souls 3.



            These paired daggers are the preferred weapons of the brigands of a distant land.

            When two-handed, the wielder holds a blade in each hand, allowing for divergent attacks that include left-handed moves.

            Skill: Quickstep
            Instantly step behind or around the side of foes. Especially effective when locked on to target.

             

            Notes and Tips:

            • Can be Infused and Buffed.
            • Reinforced with Titanite.
            • All weapons have motion values assigned to each attack, which are modifiers to AR that are associated with the attack being used.
              • The Brigand Twindaggers have a motion value of 375 on backstab, 450 on riposte, and 75+437.5 on Hornet Ring riposte. The motion value of a backstab with a dagger does not change with Hornet Ring.
              • This means that aside from a difference in AR, no infusion is strictly better for ripostes, as all infusions will get the same motion value, with the only difference being AR. Use whatever infusion will net the most damage for the best results.
              • Note that Dark damage does not benefit from Hornet Ring in PvP.
            • When paired, this weapon has incredibly synergy with the Pontiff Eye Rings and similar effects.
            • A good PvP combo for this weapon is R1+L1+L1. 
            • The Brigand Twindaggers have a range 23.5671xl on the 1h R1 and 24.614xl on the 2h L1.
            • The Weapon Skill Quickstep can be used to lessen the recovery frames of any attack.
            • Compatible with Legion Etiquette gesture when equipped on left hand.



            Location/Where to Find

            • Looted from a corpse where you also find the Brigand Set after the hostile NPC at Road of Sacrifices.

Over-reliance on Quick Step can result in your opponent catching the Instability Frames that occur after each Quick Step if you just spam it and become predictable. The twindaggers certainly require a very aggressive strategy, but if you aren't able to out-think your opponent, you'll find yourself parried, out-spaced, or just out-damaged.
