            Ringed Knight Straight Sword is a Weapon in Dark Souls 3. It is part of The Ringed City.



            Straight sword wielded by the Ringed Knights.
                     The arms of early men were forged in the Abyss, and betray a smidgen of life.

            For this reason the gods cast a seal of fire upon these swords, and those who possessed them.

            Skill: Ember
            Stand ready with the timeworn blade as it restores its ancient brilliance and reignites a short-lived flame. From this stance transition to normal or strong attack.

             

            Notes and Tips:

            • Cannot be Infused or Buffed.
            • Reinforced with Twinkling Titanite.
            • As this weapon shares its one-handed moveset with the Longsword, an effective combo for PvP is R1+R1+R2. While it doesn't true combo, the delay of the R2 lines up with the duration of a medium roll, letting the attack connect outside of the opponent's i-frames.
            • All weapons have motion values assigned to each attack, which are modifiers to AR that are associated with the attack being used.
              • The Ringed Knight Straight Sword has a motion value of 200 on backstab, 250 on riposte, and 60+225 on Hornet Ring riposte. The motion value of a backstab with a straight sword does not change with Hornet Ring.

             

            • The Ringed Knight Straight Sword has a range of 36.3408xl on the 1h R1 and 35.8932xl on the 2h R1
            • The Ringed Knight Straight Sword has thrusting R2 attacks.
            • The recovery of all attacks can be canceled by quickly pressing the Weapon Skill button during the recovery frames of an attack.
            • All parts of the weapon art are parryable.
            • While holding the weapon art, movement is slowed to a walk, and stamina regen is greatly reduced.
            • The Weapon Art R1 and R2 can be used immediately after activating the Weapon Skill, before the flame expands from the sword.
            • When you run out of FP, the sword will still ignite while holding the Weapon Skill button, but not when you swing it.
            • The weapon art adds a Flat 90 Fire damage increase to AR

             

             

            Location/Where to Find

            • The Ringed City: From the Ringed City Streets bonfire, head out into the swamp and head straight, past several Locust Men. Defeat or run by the judicator here and bank right, and up a small hill. It is here laying on a corpse near a Locust Man.

                  Glitching aside, this thing is stupid good iv PvP. Sure, it's not buffable, is entirely inflexible to a build other than Quality or Strength, and has relatively modest damage. But the weapon art more than makes up for those shortcomings. Specifically, it grants very fast, and very strong hyperarmor. The weapon art on this STRAIGHT SWORD, that costs a mere 7 FP to use, has better poise and trading potential than a CURVED GREATSWORD. Oh, and the weapon art also hits multiple times, meaning that you are very, very likely to hit your opponent in between hyperarmor if they're trading with you, guaranteeing you the rest of the combo. Oh, and it extends the range of the sword, making it easier to outspace your opponents. Oh, and the R2 immediately out of weapon art is an outstanding rollcatch. The sword itself isn't so bad either, boasting a long length and a tiny amount of fire damage to finish off a BKS turtle after you pop their tears.

                                                                                                                            Ringed Knight Straight Sword 101 - The scaling of this weapon is a bit deceiving, as the B for STR and D for DEX would lead you to believe this is a straight sword exclusively for strength builds. However, unless you are well under level 100, you're going to want to level DEX as well as STR. 40 STR is a must, as for DEX: 30, 33, and 37 are the ideal stopping points. While two handing this weapon is tempting because of the high STR scaling and to have access to the unique weapon art, this weapon truly shines when one handing it with a "weapon skill" shield. Ethereal Oak Shield is a great choice. This allows you to have access to the quick, low stamina usage move set of one handing a straight sword as well has being about to use weapon art on the fly. So you don't have to delay and telegraph to your opponent that you are going to use the weapon art. As with all straight swords, you're going to want to follow up a typical one handed R1, R1 combo with an uncharged R2. This will hit your opponent somewhat frequently with very little risk to yourself. The weapon art has a true combo (a series of hits that are inescapable in PVP every time if done correctly... and without lag): weapon art R1 into another weapon art R1. Be aware that the weapon art can be used almost immediately after holding L2, you do not need to wait for the animation to finish. Similarly to the basic R1, R1 true combo, you're going to want to follow up the weapon art R1, weapon art R1 true combo with an R2 most of the time. A regular R2, not a weapon art R2. This will hit your opponent somewhat often and is, again, very low risk. The weapon art R2, while useful in some situations, should be used sparingly and it's follow up R2 even more sparingly. Using the weapon R2 instead of a regular R2 after the weapon art R1, weapon art R1 true combo can catch your opponent off guard occasionally, but doesn't hit as consistently and has some risk. The greatest draw back to this weapon is its damage output. It's not as powerful as other straight swords can be and it doesn't fit with most builds. However, the diverse move set the weapon art allows compensates for this. This is definitely a great weapon to consider if it fits your build and you find yourself bored with the simplicity of other straight swords.
