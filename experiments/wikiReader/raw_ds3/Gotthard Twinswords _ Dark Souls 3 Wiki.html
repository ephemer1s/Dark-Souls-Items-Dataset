            Gotthard Twinswords is a Weapon in Dark Souls 3.



            The hunters known as the King's Black Hands wielded paired weapons. These belonged to Gotthard, who fled the castle.


            Gotthard rose quickly through the knighthood, no surprise to those who have witnessed his swordsmanship.


            Skill: Spin Slash
            Slice into foes with a large spinning motion, and use strong attack to lengthen attack and end with a double-bladed thrust.



            Notes and Tips:

            • Can be Infused and Buffed.
            • Reinforced with Titanite.
            • All weapons have motion values assigned to each attack, which are modifiers to AR that are associated with the attack being used.
              • The Gotthard Twin Swords have a motion value of 200 on backstab, 250 on riposte, and 60+225 on Hornet Ring riposte. The motion value of a backstab with a straight sword does not change with Hornet Ring.
            • The Gotthard Twinswords are the longest 1h straight sword, with a range of 36.4043xl on the 1h R1, as well as 42.1088xl on the 2h L1.
            • The Gotthard Twinswords have thrusting R2 attacks.
            • As a Paired Weapon, this weapon can't be two-handed, and will instead gain a new moveset mapped to the L1 button.
            • The weapon is popular in PVP for its long reach and its L1 to Weapon Art true combo.



            Location/Where to Find

            • Can be found on Black Hand Gotthard's corpse just outside the Grand Archives. You must have defeated Aldrich, Yhorm, and the Abyss Watchers or this sword will not be there.

                                                                                                                                              Remember kiddos, to avoid parries: delay your rolling attacks, or simply don't use them. Only R1 twice if you hit them, then charge your R2 a second to roll catch your opponent or punish their parry. if your first R1 hits a shield, go into a charged R2 as it's a parry bait. Slways mix up your R2 charging, make some short, some long, never charge so long it auto strikes. Never let your oppenent get your timing down for those charged R2's. Learn your spacing for L1, as it's a slower attack that is hard to use offensively, best used only if you know your opponent whiffed his attack.
