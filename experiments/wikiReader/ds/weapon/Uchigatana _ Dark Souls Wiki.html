            Uchigatana is a Weapon in Dark Souls and Dark Souls Remastered.

             

            Katana forged in an Eastern land. Known for its brisk slashing motions."
            "The Uchigatana cuts beautifully and causes bleeding, but its blade is easily nicked."

            How to Get / Where to Find the Uchigatana

            • Dropped by the Undead Merchant in the Undead Burg.
            • Sold by Shiva of the East for 5,000 souls.

            Notes:

            • Give to Frampt to receive 50 souls.
            • Bleed build up is 33 per strike, and once inflicted will tear 30% of the opponents HP.
            • Resembles more of a tachi, as the blade seems longer than that found in most standard uchigatana.
            • Demon's Souls version: Has the same appearance and move set, but has greater range than in Dark Souls.

                                          Ok, I'd like to start a conversation about this weapon. Between Uchi vs. Iatio, I can argue that the Uchigatana is a *slightly* better choice in general. At pretty much any dex level, the Uchi has more AR for normal reinforcement, although barely much more at all. The lower dex requirement can win some people over too. But most importantly I think is the difference in the heavy attack and the damage type(s). The Uchi has a piercing attack, while the Iaito is limited to slash attacks. That piercing attack can mean the difference of having to switch to another weapon or not for certain enemies and player armors, and I think this is the Uchigatana's strongest attribute. However, I wouldn't shame anyone who chooses the Iaito over the Uchi, the overall advantage is under generalized conditions, and not by a terrible amount.
