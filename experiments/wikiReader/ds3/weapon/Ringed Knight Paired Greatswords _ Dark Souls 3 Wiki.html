            Ringed Knight Paired Greatswords is a Weapon in Dark Souls 3. It is part of The Ringed City.



            Paired black greatswords wielded by the Ringed Knights.
            The arms of early men were forged in the Abyss, and betray a smidgen of life. For this the gods cast a seal of fire upon such weapons, and those who possessed them.

            Skill: Ember
            Cross the twin timeworn greatswords to reignite a short-lived flame. A lightning-quick upward heave slices the very air, and transitions into normal or strong attacks.

             

            Notes and Tips:

            • Cannot be buffed or infused.
            • Reinforced using Twinkling Titanite 
            • This weapon is extremely powerful if you successfully combo the L1 attacks.
            • The weapon skill starts off with a diagonal strike which then unleashes a short fiery projectile and can transition into an overhead leap R2 or a powerful thrusting attack with R1.
            • Acts much like the Farron Greatsword in which you can activate the L1 attacks anytime during a R1/R2 combo and vice versa.
            • This weapon is the only Paired Ultra Greatsword of the game.
            • Has a higher poise health value than other Ultra Greatswords. While all the other Ultra Greatswords have a poise health value of 27.3, the RKPGS has 30.8 poise health. This means you can 39.3 poise to tank through other Ultra Greats instead of 45.9. All of this applies to the Fume Ultra Greatsword, since it also has 30.8 poise health.



            Location/Where to Find

            • The Ringed City: Drops from Ringed Knight carrying them just before the cathedral, right near the altar where you rank up the Spears of the Church.

                                                              A couple strategies I've noted since picking up the weapon is that you really can't throw out an L1 or an R1 from neutral; L1's only land in neutral if your opponent fails to react entirely and the R1's are parryable.
                                                              Second R1 combos into the first if the first one misses, and the 3rd combo of the L2 is amazing at roll catching and can be thrown after a successful second R1. L1 is only good for chasing; does not true combo into a second L1 if you made any contact with your opponent first for the hitstun, which is nice.
                                                              L2/WA has very fast hitstun for anyone unfortunate yo be in your face and true combos into the R2 followup (front flip explosion) which is very fun and reliable to pull off. WA and the followups have absolutely no tracking and *need* to be manually aimed as the projectile almost always hits the ground first - even in flat arenas such as pontiff's.

                                                                  Both this and the Farron Greatsword work well as "blenders" with lots of hyperarmor, damage, and stun potential. Everything they did right to balance the FGS, though, they did wrong with this thing.

                                                                  FGS requires careful positioning to hit with the offhand knife if you want an early stagger that avoids parries. If the user doesn't use it properly, they get parried which not only does a lot of burst damage, it grants the opponent i-frames preventing teammates from defending the user from the consequences of their mistake. RKGS startup grants near immediate stunboxes, covers a long distance, and has very good tracking for how big its hitbox is, both in width and length. Plus, it is not parriable despite being one handed UGSs, which by all other examples in the game are parriable. As a result spamming the L1 is easy, effective, and difficult to punish. (A parriable startup to this would be well enough to balance this weapon, and there's no reason for it not to be parriable.)

                                                                  As to fighting this, if you're 1v1 it's not that bad. You can simply dance around them if the connection is good, or you can stomp/persevere. A well timed R2 from the Great Club or Crucifix of the Mad King work well to step in at the end of the L1 before they start their next movement. A charged one will often knock them over, even. If you have high poise, repeatedly smashing the Crucifix's L1 also works pretty well.

                                                                  The usual problem with fighting this weapon that it's part of a gank squad. Honestly, just bring a high stability shield and backstab fish. If you circle in with the shield up, you avoid stun from the L1 startup and they usually end up running past you. Fire dagger at low levels, dark build with chaos dagger at higher levels, hornet ring obviously. Light rolling helps get past the huge hitbox and behind them if they start the attack at distance. It's the same as fighting any other UGS, just with a more annoying WA.
