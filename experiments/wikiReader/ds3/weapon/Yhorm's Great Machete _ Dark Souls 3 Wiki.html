            Yhorm's Great Machete is a Weapon in Dark Souls 3.



            Great machete wielded long ago by Yhorm the Giant.

            Yhorm once lumbered on the frontlines with a greatshield. But one day, in place of his shield, a left-hand notch was added to his machete, enabling the smashing technique that would become the legacy of his later years.

            Skill: Warcry
            Let out a spirited warcry that temporarily boosts attack, and enables a crushing strong attack. 



            Notes and Tips:

            • Cannot be infused or buffed. 
            • Reinforced using Titanite Scales.
            • At 66 STR, Yhorms Machete +5 AR is 650. When two handed it gets 707 AR, highest number of all physical damage weapons. 
            • Has the highest range out of all the Greataxes.
            • Neither this nor Great Machete have a guaranteed combo while two handing. Its damage coupled with its length might have been considered too efficient, as is the case with the Washing Pole, and thus they decided to exchange the two-hit combo for hitbox size.
            • Unlike other war cry weapons, there is no variation in the animations between one-handed and two-handed heavy attacks after war cry has been used.

             

             

            Location/Where to Find

            • Crafted via Soul Transposition from Soul of Yhorm the Giant

                                                                                      Here's my deep opinion on this weapon as this weapon is probably one of the most unique weapons in the game for small reasons.

                                                                                      DAMAGE: When having 66strgth with this machete, you get a maximum of(including war cry) 750AR or somewhere around that range. That's a ALOT of AR for just a Great Hammer. On average, against Havel monsters, you'll hit for about 400 damage which to say the least is amazingly strong. People who wear cloth-like armor will take 100 - 300 more damage, which.. by the way... if they have a Damage Absorption of 10 - 18%, you'll guaranteed take 600 damage. So, it's a con that this weapon is dependent on your opponent's armor but at least some of the best DA(Damage Absorption) sets still can't handle this things damage. But a con I want to mention is that you're not guaranteed 2 hits from this weapon since it hits as hard as Long Island.

                                                                                      RANGE: My.... god... the range is insane for this thing. I would go as far as saying it's 3/4th the range of Dragonslayer Spear's range. It's really that much range. Now... I'm talking about the weapon's OVERALL range. This weapon has an awkward hitbox. The hitbox seems like it doesn't full reach ALL the way to the end of its' blade, more like half way. The rest of its' blade, the more tipper-part is where the pure shockwave comes from. So, for everyone wondering why its' shockwave is gigantic, this is the reason. This is a extreme benefit because for most slow weapons, when you swing, people love to punish them- BUT, for this weapon. HAH, NOPE. The shockwave will save you so many times from being punished.

                                                                                      WEAPON ART: I see people saying this weapon art sucks really badly. I'll gladly disagree. Dont underestimate this weapon's art because not only do you gain a good chunk of poise when attacking no matter how much you charge it but the unique style of attacking while running is what makes it amazing. On average, against somebody who has under 20% DA, you'll deal 600 damage. Over 21%, 500 damage. But, here's the thing though. That's your initial fully charged swing of the WA. Not the second(where you slide the machete against the ground and you slam it down like the Butcher's Knife). If you land the second WA hit, you'll deal even more damage ranging from 800 damage(under 20% DA). Dang man, that's a lot!


                                                                                      OVERALL: I recommend to use this weapon for PvP as it covers most things that you'll need throughout your PvP; damage, range, great WA. But major cons are: Stamina consumption, awkward split hitbox, slow, decent hyper armor for every swing excluding WA swings. The pros definitely out way the cons for sure. If you disagree, don't feel bad for disliking but if you agree, that's great. Bye.
