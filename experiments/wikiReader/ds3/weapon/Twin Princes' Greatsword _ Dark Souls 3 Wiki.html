            Twin Princes' Greatsword is a Weapon in Dark Souls 3.



            Sword forged from the reunion of the inseparable swords born from the souls of Lothric and Lorian, the twin princes.

            Their union is rooted in a curse, and perpetuated by grief.

            Skill: Sacred Light and Flame
            While in stance, use normal attack to cast Sacred Lothric Light, and strong attack to cast Flame of Lorian. 

            Notes and Tips:

            • Cannot be Buffed or Infused. 
            • Reinforced with Titanite Scale.
            • The Twin Princes' Greatsword has a range of 34.1973xl on the 1h R1 and 37.0217xl on the 2h R1.
            • At 40 Str 40 Dex 9 Int 9 Faith this weapon has an AR of 540.
            • At 40 Str 14 Dex (minimum) and either 40 Int 9 Faith or 9 Int 40 Faith this weapon has an AR of 521.
            • At 22 Str (minimum) 40 Dex and either 40 Int 9 Faith or 9 Int 40 Faith this weapon has an AR of 537.
            • At 22 Str and 14 Dex with 40 Int and 40 Faith this weapon has an AR of 518.
            • At 40 Str, 40 Dex, 40 Int and 40 Faith this weapon has an AR of 612. This weapon scales poorly with marginal increases in any of the four stats past the 40 softcap.
              • The above stat investments are at +5 refinement using the Knight class. Without heavy investment in all four damage stats this weapon falls short in damage relative to other greatswords; even so, it has a decent AR regardless of how you invest points since it scales well with all stats up to the 40 softcap. In combination with its unique and powerful weapon arts, it remains an effective weapon for any build.

            Location/Where to Find

            • Requires Lothric's Holy Sword and Lorian's Greatsword, both can be obtained from Ludleth of Courland via Soul Transposition.
            • Barring item trades with other players Lothric must be defeated twice (NG and NG+) in order to obtain this weapon.
            • Ludleth will only offer to merge the two swords if both are in the player's main inventory when speaking to him. This can be done even if Ludleth has been slain.

                                                                                                                              This is easily one of the better greatswords, despite having mildly poor AR (split damage weakens its output), because of the usual GS pros (HA on R1s + reasonably fast R1s = ability to trade with fast weapons; great at chaining roll catches) and, more importantly, it's ability to pressure an opponent with the WA R1: it's stupid fast, has dual hit boxes (thrust + beam), has crazy good range, and roll catches after R1s so well. Keep in mind, it's pretty taxing FP-wise and the beam is all physical damage, so 100% shields shut it down (and this weapon's nemesis happens to be one of the most popular medium shields in the game as it counters both it's physical and fire damage.) As a side note (HINT HINT), this weapon gets better FAITH scaling (+2 per point) than INT so I usually go 26 STR 40 DEX 30 FAITH, and then have a Lightning-infused Corvian Greatknife or Mail Breaker (as they both get really good scaling on that infusion) as a side arm to get around those pesky shields. The WA R2 is shockingly good for not just tanking ultra GS swings, but for quickly resetting your poise IN THE MIDDLE OF A TRADE. All this leads me to believe this is a true skill weapon, and you can do some scary good work with it.
