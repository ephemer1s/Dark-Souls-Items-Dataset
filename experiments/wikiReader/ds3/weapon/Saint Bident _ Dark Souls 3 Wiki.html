            Saint Bident is a Weapon in Dark Souls 3.



            A silver bident decorated by a holy symbol, formerly wielded by Saint Klimt. He discarded this weapon, that draws upon one's faith, on the day that he put his own faith behind him.

            Skill: Charge
            Hold spear at waist and charge at foe. Use strong attack while charging to extend the length of the charge.



            Notes and Tips:

            • Can be Infused and Buffed.
            • Reinforced with Titanite.
            • Resembles the weapon wielded by Archangel Avacyn, a character from the trading card game "Magic: the Gathering"
            • It's actually longer than the Pike while still having the same moveset as standard spears (its normal and heavy attacks are much faster than pikes). 
            • Faith scaling was buffed in regulation 1.32. Now has C faith scaling at regular +10 compared to D scaling pre-patch.
            • With 60 Faith and at base requirements for Strength and Dexterity with the Lightning infusion the AR is 504, +10 uninfused with Lighting Blade the AR reaches 552.



            Location/Where to Find

            • Found at Cathedral of the Deep: next to one of the chained-Giants, there is a big blue door that leads to the area where this is found.
            • Watch the Fextra Video at 2hr 31mins 31secs in.

                                                                                                                                                                          Physical faith scaling, and can be infused/buffed. A real oddity.About average damage by itself, if a little below average, even with high FTH. Whilst all of its scaling is D, the FTH scaling is slightly higher, so if you're going to use this, pump dat faith.I wouldn't bother infusing it with anything; Heavy is bad for this, Sharp is bad, Refined is bad, Raw is bad. Blessed is absolutely worthless, actually lowering the faith scaling to an even lower D grade. If you are going to infuse it with anything, Lightning and Dark are the only decent infusions, but these introduce split-damage to the weapon and hamper the already low base damage, overall lowering its quality.It's much better to leave this weapon uninfused, and just buff it with Lightning Blade, Blessed Weapon, Dark Blade, or Darkmoon Blade. You will get far better results.In terms of the weapon itself in combat, it's extraordinarily long for a regular spear, it's as long as the Pike-style spears, but has the regular spear moveset. It has a rather specific sweet-spot just before the end of the spear, but not at the end of the spear, it's strange, and can hamper the weapon in combat when keeping an enemy at the optimal range can be difficult. The R2 attacks ignore the sweet-spot and deal full damage regardless of range. The R2s also have some innate shield-piercing, even a 100% block greatshield will take 100~200 damage per blocked charge-R2, depending on your AR.The spear does have very low base damage though, and is quite heavy. Don't expect to pick this up and immediately get better results than the Deep Axe or something else available so early in the game. It takes a while before it gets going.
