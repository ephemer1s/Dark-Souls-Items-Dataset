            Golden Ritual Spear is a Weapon in Dark Souls 3.



            A ritual spear presented to Darkmoon Knights before Sulyvahn claimed the title of Pontiff.

            Can also be used as a staff. Sorceries cast using this weapon channel the wielder's faith.

            Skill: Steady Chant
            Boost the strength of sorceries for a very short period. 

            Notes and Tips:

            • Cannot be Infused or Buffed. 
            • Reinforced with Twinkling Titanite.
            • Doubles as a Catalyst that scales with Faith.
            • Has the shortest range out of all the Spears and Pikes.
            • Wielded by Aldrich, Devourer of Gods.
            • At 60 FTH it has a spellbuff of 220.

            Location/Where to Find

            • Dropped from a Mimic just above Pontiff Sulyvahn bonfire. (Video)

                                                                                            If you play it right you have a spear, longsword *with roll attacks,* a sweeping magic aoe (greatsword, old moonlight), a projectile weapon and aoe-generator (pestilent mercury, snapfreeze, etc.) ALL IN ONE HAND. Bonus points in using a weapon-art shield and you can choose to either 'simple' it or use Great Magic Shield to turn even the buckler into the stat-equivalent of a greatshield.

                                                                                            The downside is that you are invested to the weapon and precisely what you want to cast, as your personal level cap decision (or lack thereof) very distinctly limits you. It's important to build how you're comfortable. Aren't good at rapidly switching spells? Keep the attunement simple and keep the spells you're good with. Prefer to use Wolf's Greatshield and thus need 30 str? Fashion Souls takes its toll sometimes and you're probably going to sacrifice Int, dex, and maybe even a bit of faith. Want offhand or secondary weapons? Do you use them more than the spear? You might be better off just using a catalyst.

                                                                                            The point is you are picking up a weapon that will force you to 'find' your playstyle and adapt to it. Keep that in mind, but when you find your groove it's an incredibly fun and effective weapon.

                                                                                            Some common INT caps:
                                                                                            18: The bare minimum, but it buys you Great Heavy Soul Arrow, all the best utility spells (aural, hidden), snap freeze, Great Magic Shield, and the frankly-op Cast Light.
                                                                                            23: Farron Flashsword and Great Farron Dart. Excellent mixups, stab/swipe with spear then flashsword, GFD is a great chip tool and a quick little slap from a distance.
                                                                                            25: Old Moonlight. Looks badass, but also a combination of MLGS and Soul Greatsword in a convenient package and hits like a truck.
                                                                                            30: Stretching the boundary of 'why not just be a regular hybrid caster' gets you Crystal Magic Weapon (not really applicable, but good to know), Homing Crystal Soul Mass, Pestilent Mercury, and Dark Edge. Don't knock Dark Edge; it's an incredibly-squirrely tool when used without lock-on and can make some very sharp tracking turns. Great poke &gt; Slam. Pestilent Mercury is surprisingly useful for a variety of encounters. For example: Aural decoy + pestilent mercury can basically trivialize entire swaths of the game and has the bonus of killing stuff instead of just running past it, and PM is great for doing tons of damage (by %!) to lots of big stationary enemies.
                                                                                            32: Affinity and Soul Spear. If you're at this point, it's because you wanted Affinity. Great Heavy Soul Arrow is cheaper FP wise and hits almost as hard as the spear; the spear just pierces targets. You've also sacrified an extra 12 points of INT in what might be a 120-150 build that are probably cutting back on your Faith total.

                                                                                            Some things to remember:

                                                                                            - 20 dex + sage ring gets you max cast speed, and the spear doesn't actually gain all that much phys damage in comparison. If you're going low-INT it's worth it to push DEX up to 40, but the strength of the spear is in its mixups and casting. You're going to basically choose your balance and it might take a respect or two to find the right 'spot.'

                                                                                            - Faith is your power stat: If you're going to sacrifice, do it from Faith *last.* The magic dmg of your spear, and your spell buff, are directly attached to this stat. You're going to want to go into this planning for 60 Faith whether you plan on using miracles or not.

                                                                                            - Just because I talked about sorceries doesn't mean that's all you can cast! It's just the way to make the most of the spear; carrying a talisman for an offhand Lightning Stake isn't a bad idea as it gives you a self-aoe nuke and a different damage type. You're also in the market for pyromancies of your choosing.

                                                                                            - Good offhand options for other builds work here too: Simple caestus, small shields, and offhand weapons synergize well with your stat requirements and the counter damage of the spear can hit like a truck. Weapon shields buffed with simple (East Iron shield is good one at 3 weight with 'weapon skill') give you the option to buff your spear, and buff your shield or constantly regenerate small amounts of FP. You can poke from behind a shield with great magic shield on and laugh when your target shield is basically an impenetrable wall for about 30 seconds. Your options are numerous, effective, and varied.

                                                                                            - Fashion Souls.

                                                                                            - Flashsword makes your spear into a hybrid magical longsword. Don't just spam the standing attack; you can do backsteps and regular attacks to thrust, or switch it up to a running sweep with Farron Flashsword, same with rolls and running attacks. At any given time, you are one of several 'kinds' of opponents if you're fighting someone and it's going to take someone at the top of their game to beat you at the top of yours. Rollcatch with Greatsword or Old Moonlight, pelt them when they roll away with darts, if you've got the attunement you've got so many options. Experiment and you'll find some interesting ways to keep people on their toes.

                                                                                            - Let the haters hate: A weapon is as viable as you want it to be. Sure, someone rolling around naked with 66 str smashing stuff with a hammer might be "best," or they'll tell you to use the latest 'meta' build, but that's not why you're looking at this spear. I'm here to tell you it works. There are those who will tell you there are 'better options.' Objectively, they're right, but that's not the spirit of Dark Souls, is it? Many monsters, minions, and people have died to this spear; seize your opportunity to start adding to the pile.
