            Dragonslayer Swordspear is a Weapon in Dark Souls 3.



            A dragon hunting weapon from the age of the gods. The earliest form of the cross spear, serving as both a sword and a spear.

            Its owner was the Nameless King, a deific hunter of dragons. The swordspear is imbued with lightning, of which he was the heir.

            Skill: Falling Bolt

            Hold swordspear high in the air to summon fierce lightning that descends upon distant foes. 



            Notes and Tips:

            • Cannot be Infused or Buffed. 
            • Reinforced with Titanite Scales.
            • The weapon is based on line of sight, meaning that if any object stands between the player and the target the lightning will strike there instead
            • Weapon art can be parried, however, this is risky because the lighting still hits.
            • Its moveset is a mix between the Spear and Glaive movesets.
              • When one-handed:
                • Light attacks are spear
                • Running attacks are spear 
                • Rolling attacks are spear/glaive (They share one-handed rolling attacks)
                • Heavy attacks are glaive
                • Leaping attacks are spear
              • When two handed:
                • Light attacks are glaive
                • Running attacks are spear
                • Rolling attacks are spear
                • Heavy attacks are spear
                • Leaping attacks are spear

             

            Softcaps & Scaling:

            • 16/22/18 = 403 AR = Base Stats
            • 28/22/18 = 439 AR = Str Softcap
            • 16/40/18 = 466 AR = Dex Softcap
            • 16/22/28 = 412 AR = Fai Softcap
            • 28/40/28 = 511 AR = Combined Softcaps

             

             

            Location/Where to Find:

            • Obtained by Soul Transposition of the Soul of the Nameless King.

                                                                                                                              I played around with it a lot in both PvE and PvP (Dex/Faith build) and I've found the weapon isn't bad, but it's not great. It's very slightly above average overall, and here's why: the moveset is actually very satisfying as it can handle a lot of situations, with 1H R1 and running 1H R1 spear pokes great, the 1H R2 excellent for horizontal roll catching/cleaving (hitting multiple targets in a cone in front of you), 2H R1 for fast, off-timed (player having difficulty gauging when they need to dodge it), and solid range blows, 2H R2 nothing special; the WA can absolutely annihilate players who don't know how to dodge it yet and even catches players d*cking around and running everywhere (it'll hit your opponent even if they're sprinting), but becomes nothing more than a long-winded weapon buff (WA gives a lingering +80? lightning buff to weapon) for everyone else; and in PvE, it's moveset and WA shine as it's very flexible. However, my major criticisms are: the weapon is absurdly heavy, seriously hampering your options for back up weapons or good armor (you may be strained for points to get full damage out of this weapon, enough endurance to not gas out, and enough vigor for good armor); the damage could use a minor buff as it's a demanding weapon stat-wise, preferably in the range of 550-600 AR with all soft caps excluding the WA buff; and the 2H R1 BADLY needs a poise boost (you can't possibly convince me a 14.5 Unit weapon should be knocked out MID-sequence while Splitleaf with 13.5 Unit weight can bulldoze through 1-2 hits with similar armor). Overall, it is a fun weapon, really fun actually, definitely worth playing with, but just needs that little extra juice.
