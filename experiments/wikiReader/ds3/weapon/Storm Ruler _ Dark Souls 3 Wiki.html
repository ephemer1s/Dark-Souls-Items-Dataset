            Storm Ruler is a Weapon in Dark Souls 3



            Greatsword with a broken blade, also known as the Giantslayer for the residual strength of storm that brings giants to their knees.

            Yhorm the Giant once held two of these, but gave one to the humans that doubted him, and left the other to a dear friend before facing his fate as a Lord of Cinder.

            Skill: Storm King
            Assume stance to imbue sword with storm. Most effective when facing giants.



            Notes and Tips:

            • Cannot be Infused, but can be Buffed with Weapon Art (WA)
            • Maintaining the LT/L2 WA stance long enough will engulf the sword in a storm, buffing it. 
            • The weapon will remain buffed until the storm is projected as described below. Consequently, a player can buff their weapon and do normal light/heavy attacks with the storm imbued blade.
            • Before the blade has been imbued with the storm, while the sword is held in the stance, pressing either r1 or r2 will execute the normal stance weapon arts(lunge and the guardbreak spin respectively), and doing so will consume fp (the storm projectile does not). This is quite unkown, and such usually catches players off guard.
            • To fire the storm as a projectile, assume the WA stance and press RT/R2 immediately if the weapon is already buffed. If not, the player will have to remain in the WA stance until the buff takes effect.  
            • Reinforced with Titanite Scale.
            • At 40/40 it has 485 AR.
            • Has 10 durability and is very easy to break. Recommended to use Wood Grain Ring to increase its durability.
            • It doesn't have any stat requirement to be used, meaning that a mage without any strength and dexterity investment can still use it.
            • Contrary to its description, neither its WA nor regular attacks do extra damage to regular giants, with the exception of Yhorm himself..
            • Cannot be sold or discarded, extra copies can be stored in the bonfire stash. 
            • The Storm Ruler is a callback to the weapon of the same name in Demon's Souls.
            • Appears the design is inspired by the "Kusanagi" sword, one of the Three Imperial Regalia of Japan.
            • Storm Ruler has a range of 33.3715xl on the 1h R1 and 36.1777xl on the 2h R1.

             

             

            Location/Where to Find

            • Dropped by Siegward of Catarina
            • Looted from corpse near the rear end of Yhorm the Giant's boss room, near his throne.

                            I honestly don't think this sword is as bad as people say. Comparing the two-handed AR to a Refined Claymore, it hits 501 while the Claymore hits 524. This difference in damage isn't anything crazy, and gives a little bit of reason to use this weapon. In favor of the Claymore, you can also use buffs, but even with a buff the damage difference isn't as good as one might expect. For example, if we have a player fully decked out in Lapp's, while having all the necessary stats to be as meta as possible (ability to hold murky, pkcs, two attunement for tears, 1285 hp) this gives them around 160 standard physical defense, not counting the other varieties, along with 32 absorption due to Prisoner's Chain. This means that Claymore with Human Pine Resin kills in 6 swings on them while dealing 281 per hit, assuming tears are active. At the same time, even with less damage and no buff applied, Storm Ruler ALSO kills in 6 swings, as it hits 252 per swing, again with tears active. Now this is where Storm Ruler gets time to shine, as it has a total of 3 weapon arts. You have the signature "Excalibur-esque" attack, which can do insane damage if you hit both the blade and the wind blade. While many people claim that this attack is too hard to land, you can free aim it quite well, so applying a bit of a learning curve to a weapon doesn't make it any worse. The range of the attack is also pretty long, but not as long as it is displayed. It is roughly the length of the Storm Ruler x3. But aside from that weapon art, you also have access to both the standard greatsword stance weapon arts, which are pretty decent as well. Additionally in the moveset, you have access to the overhead strike for your rolling attack, which is leaps and bounds better than Claymore's rolling poke. Only thing Claymore has on this is the moveset department is access to the R2 poke, but I don't think the swipe is bad either. All of these little things add up in my opinion to making Storm Ruler possibly a greatsword contender for me when using a quality build. If I wanted a greatsword to do the most damage, I would use Greatsword of Judgment, as that has by far the highest AR anyways.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                    Looks like the table for the damage on this thing is a little outdated. At +5, it's now 280 base damage, 485 with 40/40 Str/Dex. Still not fantastic, but not horrible either.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                    It has the same moveset as the Executioner's Greatsword. Outside the boss fight, its Weapon Art is essentially Stance with the same follow ups and can charge for a semi-ranged attack that does about around 2.5x damage at 2.5x it's regular range. Interestingly, the charge stays until you use the Weapon Art and persists even after using a bonfire. It's actually pretty fun to do a Storm Ruler-only run, whacking people with a tornado sword, so even if it's not top tier, it's definitely a viable and interesting weapon.
