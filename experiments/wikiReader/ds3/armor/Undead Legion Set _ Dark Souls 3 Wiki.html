            Undead Legion Set is an Armor in Dark Souls 3.


            Attire of the Abyss Watchers, the Undead Legion of Farron.

            A black-dyed leather vest worn over chainmail. (Chest)
            Black-dyed leather tied around the arms, with only the left fitted with a gauntlet. (Gauntlets)
            The iron kneecaps originated from their sword techniques. (Leggings)

            These undead warriors vowed to partake of wolf blood. They acted in the dark, seeking out any sign of the Abyss, fighting a constant war with its abominations. This pointed steel helm was a distinct symbol of the Legion, shirked as a sinister omen by the masses.

            Location/Where to Find

            • Buy from Shrine Handmaid in Firelink Shrine for 31000 souls after defeating the Abyss Watchers.

            Notes and Tips

            • This armor set is one of the best armors regarding its stats. The defence/weight rate is really good.
            • The Abyss Watchers stem from the Wolf Knight, who's better known as Artorias the Abysswalker.

                                                                                          If you look closely, you can see that underneath the high collar is a beaked mask, similar to a plague doctor's attire.

                                                                                          This is intriguing for two reasons:
                                                                                          1) it explains why the crystal sage hat also has a plague doctor's mask; it's simply standard attire of the legion.
                                                                                          2) it proves that the legion viewed the Abyss as a plague, and so used methods to combat it more akin to how 16th century plague doctors combatted the plague. This is similar to how the Hunters of Hunters in Bloodborne use the beak mask to stave off the curse of beasts.

                                                                                          This really makes sense when considering two things:
                                                                                          1) the lore of the abyss watchers is heavily inspired by the black plague.
                                                                                          2) the helmet of the abyss watchers itself says that it was "viewed as a sinister omen by the masses", paying homage to plague doctors as they were often viewed as messengers of death in their respective time period.
