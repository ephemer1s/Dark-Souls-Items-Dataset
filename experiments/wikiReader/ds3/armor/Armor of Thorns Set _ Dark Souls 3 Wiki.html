            Armor of Thorns Set is an Armor in Dark Souls 3.




            (Piece name) of Kirk, the notorious knight of Thorns. A dense patch of thorns grows from its surface.

            A fitting item for the murderous Kirk, for even the simple act of rolling can damage enemies when wearing this attire.





            Location/Where to Find

            • Found on a body by Rosaria's Bed Chamber.
            • You have to have been invaded by and vanquished Kirk on the ground floor of the Cathedral.
            • Do not defeat Deacons of the Deep before trying to obtain this armor.  Kirk will not invade afterwards.





            Notes and Tips

            • Wearing any piece of this armor set causes your rolling and Quickstepping to do 2 damage. On top of that, wearing additional pieces causes the damage to stack. For example, equipping two pieces will do 4 damage per roll and equipping all pieces will do 8 damage per roll.
            • Any piece of this set deals Standard Damage.
            • Damage to enemies and NPCs by falling on them is amplified by each piece of this set.
            • The leg piece of this set slightly increases the damage of kicking and the Tackle crossbow skill.
            • Useful for finishing low health enemies, or players with Tears of Denial.
            • Regardless of stats, no piece, nor combination of pieces will cause bleed damage.
            • Enemies that would normally stagger from rolling into them do not stagger while wearing this set.
            • The following is a tested list of items that do increase rolling damage: Red Tearstone Ring, Power Within, Morion Blade.
            • The following is a tested list of items that do not increase rolling damage: Flynn's Ring, Lloyd's Sword Ring, Pontiff's Right Eye ring (The armor set also does not activate Pontiff's Right Eye), Spiked Shield.
            • The amount of damage done varies based on enemy type, as well as armor.  Tested on NG+7, at High Wall, unarmored enemies were taking 15 damage.  While they don't stun in the traditional way while rolling into them, after enough rolls the enemy will in fact stun.  This stun is similar to getting enough hits on Iudex Gundyr, where they stop moving temporarily, and take extra damage for a short time.  Unarmored enemies will go as high as 21 damage per roll in this situation.  It was taking on average 38 rolls to kill the standard hollows. The knight in High Wall was taking 8 to 9 damage per roll.  Multiple enemies can be damaged per roll, assuming they are close together.

                                                                                              Someone here said that using gauntlets makes your roll deflect against bigger enemies. It's only partially true, as the case is a lot different, so I'll explain how it works. Wearing ANY part of the set when trying to roll into larger enemies will cause it to be deflected/bounced ( you can wear even leggings or helmet and it will happen ) but ONLY if you do not meet at least one single requirement on your weapon, including strength to one-hand, even if you can two-hand, or not enough faith/int/dex. Also the case seems to happen whether you wield them in right or left hand, except for bows and crossbows, when used on the latter. The effect itself happens when rolling next to most bigger enemies, including Millwood Knights, Giant Slaves, Gargoyles, Giant Crabs, Pontiff Beasts and some other. It also happens in case many bosses that are at least a few times bigger than character, starting from Pontiff.
