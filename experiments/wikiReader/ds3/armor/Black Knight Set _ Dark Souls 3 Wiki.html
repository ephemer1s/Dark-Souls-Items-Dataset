            Black Knight Set is an Armor in Dark Souls 3.




            Armor of the Black Knights who roam the lands.

            The knights served the First Lord Gwyn, and followed him into the flame upon its linking. They became ash, but still wander the realms to this day.





            Location/Where to Find

            • Dropped by the Black Knights. One of the first Black Knights is near the Farron Keep Perimeter bonfire. He can drop the entire set, in addition to the Black Knight Shield and Black Knight Greatsword. 
            • Can be farmed in the area after Champion Gundyr in the Untended Graves as it contains 4 Black Knights and is directly adjacent to the bonfire.
            • Another Black Knight is located in the Smouldering Lake near the bonfire Demon Ruins.
               





            Notes and Tips

            • Grants the third highest fire resistance for an armor set when all the pieces are worn, surpassed only by the Black Iron Set and Havel's Set.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                The most frustrating part of this armour is its helmet. It could look cool, but 1) it looks skinny on a bulky character and 2) you can put a sword right in that enormous hole between chest and helm's "visor". Second thing on the list is hand piece. If you are an OCD type, hands do not entirely match the whole set and do not look as epic as it could be. So here is a good solution: Billed Mask for helm [192.208.60.28's idea], or Lorian's Crown if you want to look more royal, and Gundyr's Gauntlets for hands. Etching, colour and the whole mood are almost perfect, for male and female characters alike. Unfortunately, only 2-3 helmets match the whole set and do not look out of place. Northern Helm could be nice, but it makes you look like an egg-head. Otherwise, 10 out of 10 for this set. Lore is what makes an armour shine, after all.
