            Sacred Bloom Shield is a Shield in Dark Souls 3.




            A treasured antique of the Way of White, known to some as the Sorcerer's Bane.

            The large blossom design that graces the shield is said to be a sacred flame, and the shield is blessed with high magic protection.

            Skill: Spell Parry

            Repel an attack at the right time to follow up with a critical hit.  Works while equipped in either hand.  Also deflects spells.





            Notes and Tips:

            • Can NOT be Infused.
            • Reinforced with Twinkling Titanite.
            • Provides an average block of 55%. Actually sports fantastic damage block for its weight. The shield also is one of two able to deflect spells, contributing to its value. This shield suffers from terrible stability however, its most glaring weakness. Compare to the Golden Wing Crest Shield.



            Location/Where to Find

            • Behined an Illusory Wall in Lothric Castle (up the ladder in the room with the Winged Knight with Twinaxes)

                                                      Spell parry works on all projectile spells, but it can struggle to parry large projectiles like some pyromancies and Great Soul Dregs, since it might not move them far enough around you for you to avoid their hitbox. It also doesn't work on "beam" spells (like Soul Stream and Fire Surge, though it will work on Fire Whip) or "crawling" spells (like Black Serpent or White Dragon Breath). It also cannot parry the AoE resulting from spells like Lightning Stake or any spell with "storm" in the name (although you can, with perfect timing, spell parry a Lightning Stake, it will not move enough to affect the damage you take). Spells cast by most enemies in PvE can be spell parried, including some boss attacks (but not including Aldrich's Soul Spear, Lothric's Crystal Soul Spear, or anything cast by Soul of Cinder), although the wisdom of doing so is often questionable. The fireballs spat by the man-serpents in Archdragon Peak also cannot be spell parried.
